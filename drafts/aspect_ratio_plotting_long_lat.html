    Aspect Ratio for Plotting Long & Lat

    Thu 03 April 2014
    Tweet Share Share Share Share

    Here’s some R code to do it:

    require(rgdal)
poly = spTransform(poly, CRS("+init=epsg:4326"))
plot(poly)
axis(1)
axis(2)

    Looks okay, right? The aspect ratio isn’t 1 here.

    What is it? Well, on the equator it would be 1, and simple trig will suggest it goes with the inverse of the cosine of the latitude. What’s the middle of your data?

    bb = bbox(polyL)
bb
         min        max
x -122.76891 -119.09532
y   51.42396   53.94661

mean(bb[2,])
[1] 52.68528

    so the ratio is:

    rat = 1/cos(52.68*pi/180)
rat
[1] 1.649441

    then:

    ggplot(polyL) +
  aes(long,lat,group=group, fill = id) +
  geom_polygon() +
  geom_path(color="white") +
  coord_fixed(ratio=rat)
